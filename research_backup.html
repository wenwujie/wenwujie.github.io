NSF Project Title:

  • EAGER: Invisible Shield: Can Compression Harden Deep Neural Networks Universally Against Adversarial Attacks?

Summary:

Deep neural network (DNN) has nowadays become the “de facto” technique adopted in a myriad of real-world applications ranging from daily object detection, game playing, medical diagnosis, to security- and safety critical driver-less cars and aircraft collision avoidance systems etc. Despite its impressive capabilities, the adversarial example-attack, of which DNN’s decision can be easily mislead with high confidence by the inputs with imperceptible perturbations, has recently emerged as a major security concern. To date such attacks are significantly threatening safety- and security-critical DNN-based cyber-physical systems, i.e. misinterpreting the “Stop” sign as a “Speed Limit” in a self-driving car, thereby causing potential disastrous consequences. Unfortunately, currently the defense techniques are significantly lagging due to limited efficiency against a variety of attacks, potential normal accuracy loss and considerable deployment complexity. The unknown adversary’s strategies in realistic scenarios further exacerbate the challenge of developing practical countermeasures. This new funded NSF project proposes to investigate a holistic “compression technique” based universal defense framework, to protect and prevent DNNs from being compromised by any types of adversarial attacks with very low cost. This project aims to create new paradigms of universally safeguarding the DNN-based intelligent systems from a fundamentally different perspective—”compression”, with focus on compressing the input feeding to DNNs and the DNN model itself. This project addresses the non-well explored territory—the defense of neural network security, from a new angle— hardware-inspired deep compression, with the goal of a low-cost universal defense framework to mitigate any adversarial attacks that are nowadays more diversified and stronger. The developed framework can work as an “invisible shield”, enabling the possibility of seamlessly integrating the defense into the necessary data and model compression procedures during DNN optimization with minimized cost. Any success from this NSF project will be revolutionary to the research community that is interested in DNN algorithms and their applications, deep learning hardware acceleration, deep learning security and image/video compression.
